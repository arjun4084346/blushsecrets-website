How to Prep for a Bridal Makeup Trial

Everything to do before your trial so your wedding-day look feels just right.

Your bridal makeup trial is a joyful, low-pressure run-through of your wedding look — a chance to sit in the chair, see the finished face in daylight, and tweak anything before the big day. A little preparation goes a long way, so here is exactly how to prep for your bridal makeup trial and walk in feeling calm and excited.

When to book your bridal makeup trial

The sweet spot is four to six weeks before the wedding. That gives you time to sit with the look, request small changes, and settle on a final plan without last-minute stress. If your date lands in the busy spring and summer wedding season here in Mountain View and the wider Santa Clara County, reach out even earlier to hold your preferred trial date. Try to schedule your trial for a morning or day when you can wear the look for several hours — it tells you how the makeup lasts through the day.

Skincare in the weeks before

Beautiful makeup starts with a healthy, hydrated canvas. In the two to three weeks leading up to your trial, keep your routine simple and consistent: cleanse gently, moisturize daily, and drink plenty of water. This is not the time to try a brand-new product, a strong chemical peel, or a first-time facial — save experiments for well before, so there are no surprise reactions. Exfoliate lightly a few days ahead to smooth away dry patches, and if your lips tend to get chapped, a nightly balm makes lipstick sit beautifully.

Gathering your inspiration photos

Pictures say what words sometimes cannot. Save a small folder of five or six inspiration photos — soft and natural, glam and glowing, a bold lip, a smoky eye — whatever speaks to you. It helps just as much to save one or two looks you do not want, so we can steer clear. If you can, choose photos of people with a similar skin tone or eye shape to yours; it makes it easier to picture the result on your own features.

What to bring to your trial

A few thoughtful items make the session smoother:

  • Your inspiration photos, saved on your phone.
  • A hint of your dress — the neckline, color, and overall vibe (a photo is perfect).
  • Any hair accessories, veil, or jewelry you plan to wear, if you have them.
  • Your own foundation or lipstick if there is a shade you already love.
  • A button-up or loose top so nothing smudges the finished look.

Communicating your vision

This is your day, and honest feedback is a gift — not something to hold back. Tell Richa how you usually wear makeup, how much coverage feels like you, and whether you want to look glowing-natural or full-glam. If something feels too heavy, too shimmery, or not quite right during the trial, say so in the moment; that is exactly what the trial is for. Snap a few photos in natural light at the end so you can revisit the look later and confirm your choice with a clear head.

Threading and brows before the big day

Well-shaped brows frame the whole face, so plan your threading around the wedding, not the trial. Book your final eyebrow threading about two to three days before the wedding — long enough for any redness to settle, fresh enough that your arches look crisp in every photo. It is a good idea to thread before the trial too, so the makeup sits on tidy brows and you see an accurate preview. Wedding makeup starts at $250, and you can see the full makeup menu anytime.

The night before — and the morning of

Get a good night’s sleep, go easy on salty food, and start the wedding morning with a clean, moisturized face. Bring your inspiration photos from the trial so the look is recreated exactly. Then relax and enjoy being pampered — you have done the prep, and the rest is ours to handle.
