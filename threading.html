What is eyebrow threading?

Threading is an ancient hair-removal technique that uses a single twisted cotton thread to lift each hair cleanly from the root. Because it works row by row, it lets Richa follow the exact line of your brow — catching even the finest hairs while shaping a shape that flatters your face. No tweezing one hair at a time, no stencils, just a fast, controlled result.

Why threading is gentler and more precise than waxing

Unlike waxing, threading never pulls at your skin and uses no hot wax or harsh chemicals. That makes it a wonderful choice for sensitive skin, for anyone using retinol or acne treatments, and for delicate areas around the eyes where waxing can irritate or lift skin. The thread only removes hair — not a layer of skin — so redness settles quickly and the finished line is crisp, defined, and natural-looking.

Threading is our specialty here, and it’s a big reason clients across Mountain View and the greater Santa Clara County keep coming back. If you’re weighing threading against waxing, our FAQ page answers the most common questions.

How long does eyebrow threading last?

Because the hair is removed from the root, most clients enjoy clean, defined brows for about 3–4 weeks before a touch-up. Regrowth comes in soft and even — not the blunt stubble you get from shaving — which makes each visit quicker and upkeep easy. Booking a regular monthly appointment keeps your brows looking their best year-round.
